Figure 2: Stacked Area Chart

The provided visualization illustrates the fluctuating landscape of terrorism from 2011 to 2021. A pronounced peak in incidents is observable around 2014, followed by a decline in subsequent years. The implications of this trend highlight potential geopolitical shifts, interventions, and changing dynamics of terror groups during this period.

Analysing specific regions, the Middle East & North Africa, alongside South Asia, stand out as areas of significant activity. These regions have faced intense conflicts, and power struggles, with the data underscoring their tumultuous nature.

The intertwined relationships of local, regional, and global forces in these areas have led to a complex web of violence, as reflected in the data.

Figure 5: Radar Graph

This circular graph provides a snapshot of attack methods used by the Taliban (blue line) and ISIS (orange line):

  • Axes: Each represents a type of attack, from Armed Assaults to Unknown methods.
  • Data Points: The distance from the center indicates frequency, with numbers showing exact incident counts.
  • Interpretation: Overlapping lines highlight shared tactics. Distinct divergences showcase unique strategies.

The graph highlights the Taliban's and ISIS's attack methods. Both favor Bombing/Explosion and Armed Assault, but while the Taliban leans towards "Unknown" tactics, ISIS often opts for Hostage Taking (Kidnapping). Distinct patterns reveal their unique strategies.

Biggest Events by Deaths

Figure 6: Horizontal Bar Chart

This horizontal bar chart provides a sobering view of the most significant terrorist events based on the number of deaths. Each bar represents a distinct event, illustrating the magnitude of casualties involved.

The colour-coded bars enable viewers to quickly identify the terrorist group responsible for each act. Events like the "Ethiopia Mai Kadra Massacre" and "Somalia's Deadliest Bombing" sit at the top, indicating their devastating impact in terms of human lives lost.

By understanding the deadliest events and the groups behind them, one can better grasp the scale and nature of global terrorist activities.
